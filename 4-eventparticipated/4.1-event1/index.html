Event 1

FCAJ Community Day

Purpose of the Event

  • Update new trends in AI, Cloud, and Generative AI.
  • Share practical experiences in deploying AI products.
  • Introduce AWS services for AI, data analytics, and content delivery.
  • Connect the community of students, engineers, and AWS enthusiasts.

List of Speakers

  • Tinh Truong – Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You
  • Anh Pham – Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick
  • Thinh Nguyen – From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation
  • Team VIB – 36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality
  • Duc Dao – Non-Determinism of “Deterministic” LLM Settings
  • Vy Lam – Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring

Session Highlights

Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

  • Explained why AI often produces inaccurate results when lacking context.
  • Introduced the evolution from Prompt Engineering to AI with memory capabilities (Memory).
  • Shared the concept of a Second AI Brain, helping AI become an intelligent assistant for long-term work support.
  • Provided advice for students starting to learn and develop AI applications.

Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

  • Quick Chat Agent: An AI assistant supporting Q&A and data analysis using natural language.
  • Quick Flows: Build AI workflows without complex programming.
  • Quick Spaces: Collaborative spaces to share knowledge and data within teams.
  • Quick Sight: Create dashboards and reports directly from data using Generative AI.

From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

  • Overview of Amazon CloudFront in content delivery.
  • Methods for cost optimization when deploying a CDN.
  • Enhance security with integrated features.
  • Improve system availability and fault tolerance.
  • Optimize performance and reduce latency for users.

36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

  • Shared reasons for joining the LotusHacks competition.
  • The process of ideation and problem definition.
  • Product development within 36 hours.
  • Challenges, failures, and lessons learned.
  • Demo of the UTMorpho product along with its future development direction.

Non-Determinism of “Deterministic” LLM Settings

  • Explained how LLMs select the next token.
  • Analyzed why Temperature = 0 still does not guarantee identical results.
  • Presented causes stemming from the inference optimization process.
  • The impact of Non-Determinism in practical AI applications.
  • Methods to mitigate variations in outputs.

Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System

  • Analyzed the differences between startup data and traditional credit systems.
  • Compared Single Agent and Multi-Agent approaches for specific problems.
  • Introduced the Virtual Credit Committee architecture.
  • Discussed Guardrails and compliance mechanisms to ensure system safety.
  • Outlined the roadmap for deploying Multi-Agent systems in enterprises.

What I Learned

AI Knowledge

  • Context-first approach: Context is more critical than the prompt itself in generating high-quality outputs.
  • Understood the development trend from traditional chatbots to AI with memory capabilities.
  • Approached the Multi-Agent model to solve complex problems.

AWS Services

  • Clearly understood the role of Amazon CloudFront in accelerating websites and APIs.
  • Learned more about Amazon Quick services that support data exploitation using AI.
  • Learned methods for optimizing cost, performance, and security on AWS.

Large Language Models

  • Understood that Temperature = 0 does not mean the output is always identical.
  • Identified the causes of Non-Determinism in LLMs.
  • Understood key considerations when building AI systems that require high stability.

Application to Work

  • Apply the Context-based AI development mindset to the AI Marketing Voice Generator project.
  • Continue using Amazon CloudFront to optimize the performance of audio and content delivery.
  • Explore more about Multi-Agent architecture to expand AI applications in the future.
  • Test LLMs with multiple runs to evaluate the impact of Non-Determinism.
  • Leverage AWS AI services to enhance development productivity.

Event Experience

Attending FCAJ Community Day was a highly rewarding experience, helping me catch up on new trends in Generative AI, Cloud Computing, and AWS services. The sharing sessions were highly practical, combining foundational knowledge with deployment experiences from community experts.

Learning from Speakers

  • Speakers shared extensive practical experience regarding AI, Cloud, and modern system architectures.
  • I gained a deeper understanding of the importance of Context when building AI applications instead of solely focusing on Prompt Engineering.
  • Gained exposure to new trends such as Multi-Agent Systems and context-aware AI.

Technical Insights

  • Better understood how Amazon CloudFront helps optimize performance, security, and costs for applications.
  • Explored the capabilities of Amazon Quick in analyzing data using natural language.
  • Understood why LLMs can still generate different results even when Temperature = 0.

Insights from the Hackathon

  • The presentation by Team VIB helped me understand the process of developing a product from an idea to a demo in just 36 hours.
  • Learned teamwork, time management, and handling difficulties during product development.

Community Networking

  • Had the opportunity to network with students, engineers, and professionals working with AWS.
  • Expanded my understanding of AI and Cloud trends being applied in enterprises.

Lessons Learned

  • AI is only effective when provided with adequate Context and appropriate data.
  • Amazon CloudFront not only accelerates content delivery but also enhances security and optimizes costs.
  • Multi-Agent Systems represent a promising trend for enterprise AI applications.
  • Practical sharing from the speakers gave me clearer direction to apply acquired knowledge to future projects and work.
